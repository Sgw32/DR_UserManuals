1          Precautions

 

1)    Always disconnect the battery before installing any version of the dashboard. It might seem obvious, and leaving the power on may feel quicker, but doing so is strictly prohibited and has already damaged several dashboards.

 

2)    Never connect an active voltage source, even through a resistor, to the sensor inputs (outside temperature, oil temperature, coolant temperature, or fuel level).

Connecting a voltage source to any input will damage the instrument panel.

3)    Gen 1 and Gen 1.5 instrument clusters do not have internal fuses. The first protective fuse sits in the vehicle's fuse box and is rated at 15A, which may be too high to protect the panel.

4)    Protect the instrument panel from direct sunlight, as prolonged exposure can degrade display quality.

5)    The brightness of Gen 1, Gen 1.5, and Gen 2 instrument clusters cannot be increased significantly because of LED and design limitations. If the display is difficult to read in bright light, shield it from direct illumination.

6)    Mechanical cable speedometers may be inaccurate at 40-60 km/h because of cable resonance.

The only remedy is to install an electronic speedometer drive, which is included with all modern Gen 1.5 and Gen 2 instrument panels.

7)    Gen 2 instrument clusters do not include a touch sensor behind the VW badge.

MFA modes can only be switched by using an external control.

8)    The Gen 2 instrument cluster draws 11-13 mA in standby mode, allowing the vehicle to sit for more than 90 days without draining the battery.

This standby consumption cannot be eliminated or reduced.

9)    None of the instrument cluster versions include an instantaneous fuel consumption gauge.

It can be added to Gen 1 and Gen 1.5 panels, but the procedure has not been tested on Gen 2 units.

Follow the installation instructions at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qWqvYc9388U.

3          Description and operation of the product

3.1        Purpose

Digifiz Replica and Digifiz Replica Next dashboards replace the original instrument panels on Volkswagen Golf, Jetta, and Scirocco second-generation vehicles while adding new functions. Their advantages include a fully digital design, LED indicators, a tachometer scale in every trim level, an integrated Bluetooth controller (Digifiz Replica), and Wi-Fi control modules (UIOD and Digifiz Replica Next expansion units).

3.2        The range of instrument panels consists of a 4-letter designation with an optional indication of the maximum engine speed supported by the device and an optional indication of the dimensions of the measured parameters for speed, temperature and fuel level.

In addition, it is permissible to indicate units of measurement using 3-letter designations.

G or D petrol or diesel

A or M assembled or for self-assembly

C or R with cable speed sensor or with electronic speed sensor

T or S pre-restyling ( CE 1) or restyling ( CE 2)

The three-letter designation consists of the letters MGFK ,

M miles

G gallons

F Fahrenheit

K Kelvin

In addition, it is possible to indicate the maximum permissible engine speed displayed by the instrument panel. For example, in the case of GACT 8, the number 8 means that the maximum speed measured by the instrument panel is 8000 rpm.

4          Operating principle

Digifiz Replica units reuse the original Volkswagen housing, connectors, a cable or electronic speed sensor, and a redesigned main board. Later versions use a newly manufactured enclosure produced with SLA technology.

The Digifiz Replica main board is a fiberglass PCB populated with discrete components. An ATMEGA 2560 microcontroller collects the data and drives the displays.

MAX 7219 seven-segment indicator drivers .

Digifiz Replica Next is built on an ESP32-S3 SoC and includes a new SLA-produced enclosure, front panel, cover, connector adapter board, and an electronic speed sensor harness. The display is illuminated by WS2812 addressable LEDs mounted behind the front frame.

5          Technical specifications

The Digifiz Replica dashboard draws no leakage current when switched off.

The Digifiz Replica Next panel draws about 13 mA from the +12 V bus while switched off, which should be considered during long parking periods.

The panel operates from the vehicle's electrical system at 9 to 16 volts DC.

The measurement specifications are identical for Digifiz Replica and Digifiz Replica Next.

The system provides measurement of:

1) Vehicle speeds

Speed is measured using a standard speedometer or speed sensor.

The absolute accuracy (systematic error) of speed measurement is 10 km/h

The relative accuracy of speed measurement is 3 km/h

The speed measurement limit is limited by mechanical values - the strength of cable and electronic speed sensors. The instrument panel provides an indication of up to 999 km / h (or mph)

2) Engine speed

The RPM data comes from the ignition signal. The distorted RPM signal comes to the optocouplers input via a special RC chain, 430 nF/1.2 kOhm, with a diode signal limiter.

Absolute (systematic error) and relative accuracy of speed measurement is 200 rpm

3) Fuel level

Fuel level data comes from a resistive sensor.

The absolute (systematic error) and relative accuracy of speed measurement is 10 liters

4) Engine coolant temperatures

The engine coolant temperature is measured via a thermistor on the engine itself using a standard harness.

Quantitative measurements of coolant temperature are not performed. Indication is performed using the indication scale.

7) Time

The time is set with an error of 1 minute.

The system should display the following indication:

1.     Direction indicators

2.     High beam signals

3.     Malfunction signal from oil pump sensor

4.     Generator fault signal

5.     Handbrake signal

6.     Signal for turning on the rear window heating (or signal for glow plug glow, only for diesel instruments.

7.     Front and rear fog light signal

7          Preparation for work and work order

7.1        Preparation for work is carried out as follows:

7.1.1      In preparation for work, it is necessary to remove the original instrument panel of the car.

7.1.2      To do this, you need to unscrew the plastic cover that covers the car's pedals and dashboard.

7.1.3      Disconnect the harness (or harness) from the instrument panel to the connector

7.1.4      Disconnect the cable sensor from the instrument panel

7.1.5      Unscrew the instrument panel from the brackets and carefully remove it from the car.

7.1.6      Pull the temperature and speed sensor harnesses (if available)

7.1.7      Install the new instrument panel into the bracket grooves and secure with screws.

7.1.8      In the case of Digifiz Replica Next, the standard MFA sensors from Volkswagen or replacements must be installed and the cables with cable lugs must be run to the standard CE 1 and CE 2 connectors.

7.1.9      To connect the steering column switch and handbrake signals on GACS / GARS / DARS / DACS (8) models, it is sometimes necessary to manually connect the MFA _ MODE , MFA _ RESET , MFA _ BLOCK cables and handbrake signals to the new instrument cluster. The required cables are labeled. Using signals from the vehicle connectors is not possible in the first revision of the instrument clusters, since the original instrument clusters used for production do not have the required contacts. In Digifiz Replica Next these signals are already connected by default to standard connectors.

7.1.10 Connect the harnesses to the instrument panel

7.1.11 Install the cable sensor (if available) into the cable sensor connector.

7.1.12 Install the dashboard panel and plastic pedal cover in reverse order.

7.2        How to work with the instrument panel:

7.2.1      The instrument panel turns on automatically when the car ignition is turned on.

7.2.2      The instrument panel lighting turns on simultaneously with the vehicle's side lights.

7.2.3      After the instrument panel is turned on, internal diagnostics of the functional components and stabilization of the mathematical model for calculating the engine speed parameter are performed. The indication is accompanied by the illumination of the entire speed scale, then the value stabilizes at the current engine speed value.

7.2.4      After the car engine is cranked up, the idle speed is indicated

7.2.5      When you start moving, the instrument panel starts displaying signals of the parameters specified in the technical specifications.

7.2.6      The instrument panel is equipped with 6 parameters measured automatically ( MFA functions)

7.2.7      The first function is to measure the daily time.

7.2.8      The second function is to measure the daily mileage.

7.2.9      The third function is measuring fuel consumption. In the first revision of the instrument panel, consumption measurement is not performed.

7.2.10 The fourth function is the average speed measurement. The average speed multiplied by 10 is displayed.

7.2.11 The fifth function is to measure the engine oil temperature via an external harness.

7.2.12 The sixth function is the measurement of the ambient temperature, carried out via an external harness.

7.2.13 If you need to switch the MFA function of the dashboard, you can switch between blocks by pressing the touch icon of the car brand on the dashboard ( Digifiz Replica , not supported on Digifiz Replica NEXT requires a steering column switch)

7.2.14 Pressing for less than 1 second toggles MFA functions

7.2.15 Pressing for 1 to 3 seconds ( if there is no steering column switch! ) switches the MFA memory blocks . The change of the block is accompanied by an indication

7.2.16 Pressing for 3 to 7 seconds resets the current MFA function . This action works for fuel consumption, daily mileage and daily time, average speed.

7.2.17 The instrument panel backlight is not adjustable, a small change in brightness is made via the brightness control above the button for turning on the parking lights and headlights (not available on Digifiz Replica NEXT )

7.2.18 The brightness of the numbers is adjusted automatically via a photodiode; if necessary, it is possible to set manual brightness control, in which case you need to set the brightness parameter via Bluetooth .

7.2.19 If you need to make changes or get data from the dashboard, you can connect via Bluetooth . See the Maintenance section.

8          Setting up and maintaining Digifiz Replica Next

This section is for panels that look like this:

8.1        The UV printed polycarbonate instrument panel plastic screen has a pattern that is easily deformed by foreign objects.

8.2        The screen must be protected from damage. In case of significant damage, contact the manufacturer for spare parts. Screen damage is not a warranty failure.

8.3        The device clock is set via the Wi - Fi control panel and is reset when the permanent power from the instrument panel is disconnected.

8.4        Configuration, data collection and other functions are carried out on Digifiz Replica Next using a web application accessible via a Wi - Fi access point or by address from a router (mobile phone).

8.5        To connect, you need to connect to the access point via Wi - Fi . Data transfer (3 G / 4 G / mobile Internet) should be disabled. The standard name of the access point created by the dashboard is Digifiz _ AP , without a password, or with the password 87654321. The name " PHOL - LABS 2" with the same password is also possible.

8.6        The standard IP address of the panel is 192.168.4.1, the web application is available at url http://192.168.4.1 . Later it is possible to change the name of the access point to connect to the one distributed by the end user's smartphone . In this case, it is then necessary to scan the subnet on the smartphone to access the panel. Most often, the address has the following pattern " 192.168. X .32", where X is a number that can be found in the IP - tools application .

10.1   The plastic instrument panel screen made of plexiglass with UV printing has a pattern that is subject to slight deformation from foreign objects.

10.2   The screen must be protected from damage. In case of significant damage, contact the manufacturer for spare parts. Screen damage is not a warranty failure.

10.3   The device contains a DS 3231 module, which contains a CR2032 battery .

10.4   The battery life is 4 years

10.5   When the battery is discharged, the real time clock is reset at each start. In this case, the battery needs to be replaced, for this it is necessary to remove the cover or both covers of the device, and without removing the harnesses, replace the CR 2032 element .

10.6   The used battery must be disposed of.

10.7   The dashboard comes with a USBasp programmer . You need to install the driver for it yourself. https://myrobot.ru/downloads/driver-usbasp-v-2.0-usb-isp-windows-7-8-10-xp.php

Or any other available link with a driver for USBasp (the authors are not responsible for the content of the link above, it is provided for advisory purposes only)

10.8   The cable to the programmer is already inserted into the dashboard as follows (check the pinout).

10.9   The programmer is separate. When connected to the programmer and inserted into the computer, the instrument panel will turn on as if it were turned on in the car. This is useful for various types of checks.

10.10   avrdude line for flashing:

avrdude -c usbasp -p m2560 -e -U lfuse:w:0xff:m -U hfuse:w:0x99:m -U efuse:w:0xff:m -U flash:w:Digifiz.ino.mega.hex

10.11   After successful (reflashing) BE SURE TO PRESS THE TOUCH BUTTON 4-5 TIMES, THIS IS NECESSARY FOR THE INITIAL FILLING OF THE MEMORY BLOCKS - on old firmware versions, in new ones this issue is resolved.

10.12   If the firmware was reflashed and the memory blocks were not filled, a second reflash is allowed, or the instrument panel can be reset using the Bluetooth command 252 0. A failure to fill the memory blocks does not lead to a breakdown of the device.
